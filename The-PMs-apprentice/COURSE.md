# PM Foundations Course — Full Syllabus

An 11-module structured learning curriculum for product managers at every level. Each module is an interactive, Socratic learning experience — not a lecture. You'll work through realistic scenarios, make real product decisions, receive scored feedback, and build genuine PM judgment.

**Total time**: 8–12 hours | **Format**: AI-assisted interactive sessions | **Command**: `/course`

---

## Course Philosophy

Most PM courses teach frameworks. This course teaches **judgment**.

The difference: a framework tells you what steps to follow. Judgment tells you which framework to use when, how to apply it to a messy real situation, and what to do when the frameworks conflict.

Judgment is built through practice and feedback — not reading. Every module in this course puts you in a realistic PM situation, asks you to make decisions, and explains *why* your decisions were strong or where they fell short. You'll do the PM's work, not watch someone else do it.

## Unit 1: Foundations

*Learn the PM role, develop the PM mindset, and practice the most fundamental PM skill: extracting real insights from users.*

### Module 1 — PM Foundations
**Command**: `/learn-foundations` | **Time**: ~45 min | **Level**: Beginner

**What you'll do**:
- Navigate a realistic PM scenario (CEO asks for a feature in 2 weeks) and learn to ask the right questions first
- Work through 3 mindset situations that reveal how experienced PMs think differently
- Complete a self-assessment across 6 skill areas and get a personalized course path

**Frameworks covered**: Output vs. outcome, problem before solution, hypothesis-driven development, the PM skill tree

**You'll know this when**: You can explain the PM role to a non-PM and distinguish outputs from outcomes in any product scenario.

---

### Module 2 — User Interview Practice
**Command**: `/learn-interview` | **Time**: ~30 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Conduct a live user interview with a simulated persona (Alex Chen, marketing manager)
- Get scored feedback across 5 dimensions: question quality, bias avoidance, JTBD extraction, interview flow, technique usage
- Practice the "last time" technique, the 5 Whys, and the Mom Test approach

**Frameworks covered**: JTBD interviewing, The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick), confirmation bias, leading vs. open questions

**You'll know this when**: You can run a 20-minute user interview without asking a single leading question and extract at least 3 distinct jobs-to-be-done.

---

### Module 3 — Continuous Discovery
**Command**: `/learn-discovery` | **Time**: ~45 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Analyze 2 interview excerpts for TaskFlow (project management tool) and identify 7 distinct opportunities
- Build an Opportunity Solution Tree: group parent and child opportunities, select the best one, justify the choice
- Map assumptions for a proposed solution across desirability, viability, and feasibility
- Design a lean experiment to test the riskiest assumption in under 1 week

**Frameworks covered**: Opportunity Solution Tree (Teresa Torres), the Product Trio model, assumption mapping, lean experimentation, build-measure-learn

**You'll know this when**: You can turn a raw set of interview notes into a structured OST and design an experiment to test your riskiest assumption before writing a single line of code.

---

**Unit 1 Checkpoint**: Can you identify the difference between a user's stated request and their underlying job-to-be-done? Can you build a simple OST from interview data?

## Unit 2: Strategy

*Learn how to define success, make trade-off decisions, and set a product direction that actually wins.*

### Module 4 — Product Metrics
**Command**: `/learn-metrics` | **Time**: ~45 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Design the full metrics system for Connectly (B2C professional social app)
- Choose and score a North Star Metric using 7 evaluation criteria
- Design 3–5 input metrics and 2–3 counter-metrics
- Receive a final scorecard with grades for each metric

**Frameworks covered**: North Star Metric (7 criteria), the 3 Business Games (Attention, Transaction, Productivity), leading vs. lagging indicators, Goodhart's Law, counter-metrics

**You'll know this when**: You can propose and defend a North Star Metric for any product, explain what Goodhart's Law means for metric design, and identify the difference between a leading and lagging indicator.

---

### Module 5 — Prioritization Frameworks
**Command**: `/learn-prioritization` | **Time**: ~50 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Apply RICE, ICE, and Opportunity Score to a realistic 8-feature backlog for Notely
- Discover where the three frameworks give different answers — and what the divergence reveals
- Learn when to use each framework and how to present prioritization decisions to stakeholders

**Frameworks covered**: RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort), ICE (Impact, Confidence, Ease), Opportunity Score (Dan Olsen), HiPPO effect, opportunity cost

**You'll know this when**: You can apply RICE scoring to a real backlog, explain why two valid frameworks might give different answers, and defend a prioritization decision to a skeptical stakeholder.

---

### Module 6 — Product Strategy
**Command**: `/learn-strategy` | **Time**: ~60 min | **Level**: Advanced

**What you'll do**:
- Build a 3-year product strategy for MindBridge (B2B wellness app) using the Playing to Win cascade
- Make choices at all 5 levels: Winning Aspiration → Where to Play → How to Win → Capabilities → Management Systems
- Run the Coherence Test on your strategy to find and fix contradictions
- Reverse-engineer a competitor's implied strategy

**Frameworks covered**: Playing to Win (Roger Martin + A.G. Lafley), Porter's Generic Strategies, Blue Ocean Strategy, competitive moats (network effects, switching costs, data advantages, brand), Coherence Test

**You'll know this when**: You can build a 5-choice strategy cascade, run the Coherence Test, and explain why strategies fail when the choices don't reinforce each other.

---

**Unit 2 Checkpoint**: Can you define a North Star Metric with 7 criteria, prioritize a backlog using RICE, and articulate a coherent product strategy using the Playing to Win cascade?

## Unit 3: Execution & Delivery

*Master the artifacts and processes that turn strategy and discovery into shipped, working product.*

### Module 7 — Product Execution & Delivery
**Command**: `/learn-execution` | **Time**: ~50 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Write a 6-section PRD for the Daily Digest feature (Notely retention scenario) section by section with scored feedback
- Convert a feature-based roadmap into an outcome-based roadmap
- Write 2 well-formed OKRs with measurable Key Results
- Run through a sprint planning simulation answering engineer edge-case questions

**Frameworks covered**: PRD structure (6 sections), outcome-based roadmaps, OKRs, acceptance criteria, sprint planning, scope management

**You'll know this when**: You can write a PRD that engineers can build from without asking you 10 clarifying questions, convert a feature list into an outcome-focused roadmap, and write OKRs that teams actually use.

---

### Module 8 — Stakeholder Management
**Command**: `/learn-stakeholders` | **Time**: ~45 min | **Level**: Advanced

**What you'll do**:
- Present Finova's Q3 roadmap to 4 simulated executives with conflicting priorities
- Navigate real-time conflict: VP Sales wants enterprise features, VP Engineering wants tech debt time, CEO wants a Series B-ready narrative, Head of CS wants retention fixes
- Receive a scored debrief on your stakeholder management performance

**Frameworks covered**: RACI model, influence mapping, "disagree and commit," executive communication, coalition-building, roadmap prioritization defense

**You'll know this when**: You can walk into a room with four executives who want four different things, present a coherent roadmap, and leave with alignment (or a clear escalation path).

---

**Unit 3 Checkpoint**: Can you write a 6-section PRD with testable acceptance criteria? Can you present a controversial roadmap decision and navigate executive pushback without losing the room?

## Unit 4: Modern PM

*Build AI-powered products and ship prototypes with AI coding tools.*

### Module 9 — AI Product Management
**Command**: `/learn-ai-pm` | **Time**: ~50 min | **Level**: Intermediate

**What you'll do**:
- Act as PM for Assist (AI-powered customer support feature on Relay)
- Choose between 3 LLM options using evaluation dimensions (accuracy, latency, cost, hallucination rate)
- Navigate 3 responsible AI scenarios (bias, data privacy, AI disclosure)
- Identify flaws in a weak system prompt and rewrite it
- Interpret a post-launch AI metrics dashboard and design an incident response

**Frameworks covered**: Model evaluation dimensions, responsible AI (bias, hallucination, transparency, data privacy, accountability), prompt engineering (system prompts, few-shot examples, temperature, prompt injection), AI metrics (output quality, safety, latency, economic), build vs. buy vs. partner, incident response

**You'll know this when**: You can frame an AI model evaluation plan, write a production-quality system prompt, and define the AI-specific metrics that would trigger an incident response.

---

### Module 10 — Vibe Coding for PMs
**Command**: `/learn-vibe-coding` | **Time**: ~30–45 min | **Level**: Beginner

**What you'll do**:
- Scope your own project idea into an achievable MVP
- Choose the right AI coding tool for your project (browser-based vs. IDE vs. terminal)
- Write a vibe spec that gives an AI coding assistant enough context to build correctly
- Plan a 3–5 session build sequence with clear checkpoints
- Learn how to review AI-generated code for quality and security issues

**Frameworks covered**: Vibe coding workflow, tool selection criteria, vibe spec writing, build sequence, code review for PMs, debugging basics

**You'll know this when**: You have a real project started in a real AI coding tool and a spec you could hand off to Claude, Copilot, or any AI assistant to continue building.

---

**Unit 4 Checkpoint**: Can you evaluate an LLM for a product feature using PM-relevant criteria? Can you write a vibe spec and start a working prototype in an AI coding tool?

---

## Unit 5: Capstone

*Apply everything you've learned in a full-lifecycle product case study.*

### Module 11 — Capstone Project
**Command**: `/learn-capstone` | **Time**: ~2 hours | **Level**: Advanced

**What you'll do**:
- Act as the lead PM for Pando (async-first B2B collaboration startup) with a goal of reaching $500K MRR
- Phase 1: Analyze 4 user interview excerpts and identify growth opportunities
- Phase 2: Build a Playing to Win strategy cascade and run the Coherence Test
- Phase 3: Design a North Star Metric, input metrics, and counter-metrics
- Phase 4: Apply RICE scoring to 5 initiatives and select your Q1 plan
- Phase 5: Write a PRD for your top initiative
- Phase 6: Present to a simulated leadership team and give a go/no-go recommendation

**Every framework from all prior modules is in play.** The quality of each phase depends on the quality of the phases before it.

**You'll know this when**: You can go from raw user interviews to a justified go/no-go recommendation, defend every decision with evidence, and walk out of a stakeholder meeting with alignment — all in a single coherent product story.

## Completion

Upon completing all 11 modules, you receive a **course completion debrief** from the capstone session that includes:
- A performance summary across all 6 capstone phases
- Your 3 strongest PM capabilities (with specific evidence)
- Your 2 highest-leverage growth areas (with next-step recommendations)
- An overall PM readiness assessment (Ready to Lead / Nearly There / Growing)

## Learning Paths

### For Complete Beginners
**Time**: ~10–12 hours | **Path**: Sequential (all 11 modules in order)
→ M1 → M2 → M3 → M4 → M5 → M6 → M7 → M8 → M9 → M10 → M11

### For Career Switchers
**Time**: ~8–10 hours | **Path**: Start at M1, accelerate through familiar areas
→ M1 (required) → self-assess → skip/skim confident areas → deep-dive gaps → M11

### For PM Interview Prep
**Time**: ~5–6 hours | **Path**: Focus on interview-tested skills
→ M4 (Metrics) → M5 (Prioritization) → M3 (Discovery) → M8 (Stakeholders) → M11 (Capstone)

### For Senior PMs / CPO Aspirants
**Time**: ~4–5 hours | **Path**: Advanced modules + capstone
→ M6 (Strategy) → M8 (Stakeholders) → M9 (AI PM) → M11 (Capstone)

### For AI Product Builders
**Time**: ~3–4 hours | **Path**: AI-focused modules
→ M9 (AI PM) → M10 (Vibe Coding) → M11 (Capstone, Phases 1–5)
