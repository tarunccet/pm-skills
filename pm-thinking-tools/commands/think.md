# /think -- Collaborative Thinking Partner

Explore a problem, decision, or idea with a structured thinking partner. Get clarity through dialogue, not just answers.

## Invocation

```
/think Should we build a mobile app or improve our mobile web experience?
/think My team's velocity dropped 30% this quarter and I can't figure out why
/think I'm not sure if I should push for a platform PM role or stay in feature work
/think How should I approach the annual planning process when I've only been here 2 months?
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Context

Parse the problem and ask 1-2 clarifying questions to ensure you're addressing the real issue, not just the surface question. Identify:
- What type of problem this is (decision, ambiguity, trade-off, people, strategy)
- What constraints exist
- What's already been considered

### Step 2: Explore Perspectives

Apply the **thought-partner** skill:

- Surface assumptions the PM might not see
- Offer 2-3 distinct perspectives on the problem
- Identify the core tension or trade-off at the heart of the issue

### Step 3: Synthesize Insights

Pull the threads together:
- Name the key trade-off explicitly
- Offer a path forward with clear reasoning
- Suggest what to do next — a conversation to have, data to gather, or decision to make

```
## Reflection
[Restatement of the real problem]

## Questions to Consider
[Hard questions that sharpen thinking]

## Perspectives
[2-3 different lenses on the problem]

## The Core Trade-off
[The fundamental tension]

## Synthesis
[Path forward with reasoning]
```

## Notes

- This is for general thinking — strategy, career, team dynamics, decisions, trade-offs
- For product-specific ideation, use pm-product-discovery skills instead
- For data-driven analysis, use pm-data-analytics skills instead
- The best output comes when you share context — the more you explain your situation, the sharper the thinking
