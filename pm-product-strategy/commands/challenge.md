# /challenge -- Constructive Stress Test

Challenge an idea, proposal, or strategy by finding weaknesses, anticipating objections, and suggesting mitigations. Strengthen your thinking before others poke holes in it.

## Invocation

```
/challenge We're proposing to sunset our free tier and move to a freemium model
/challenge [paste or upload PRD] Find the weaknesses in this PRD
/challenge Our strategy is to go upmarket — what could go wrong?
/challenge I want to pitch an internal tools team — stress-test my argument
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Proposal

Read the idea, proposal, or strategy carefully. If a document is provided, review it in full. Identify:
- The core thesis — what is being proposed and why
- The key assumptions baked in
- The intended audience for the proposal

### Step 2: Identify Weaknesses

Apply the **devil-advocate** skill:

- Audit the assumptions — which are validated, which are not?
- Check the logic chain — does the reasoning hold or are there leaps?
- Find edge cases where the idea breaks down
- Assess execution risks — the three most likely ways this fails in practice

### Step 3: Anticipate Objections

Map likely pushback from each stakeholder group:
- Engineering, design, leadership, sales, finance
- Identify the single hardest objection and craft a response

### Step 4: Suggest Mitigations

For every weakness found, offer at least one concrete way to address it. Frame the output constructively — the goal is to make the idea stronger, not to kill it.

```
## What's Strong
[What works well and why]

## Key Challenges
[3-5 substantive challenges with mitigations]

## Likely Objections
[Stakeholder objection table with suggested responses]

## Overall Assessment
[Honest summary and the single most important thing to address]
```

## Notes

- This is for challenging ANY idea — product proposals, strategies, career moves, process changes
- For pre-launch risk analysis specifically, use the pre-mortem skill in pm-execution
- Upload or paste documents for the most thorough analysis
- The more context you provide, the more specific and useful the challenges will be
