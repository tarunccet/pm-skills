## Pivot Decision: CraftLocal Community Marketplace
**Date:** 2025-05-15
**Decision Stage:** Evaluation

### Current State Summary

- **Timeline:** 6 months since public launch (Nov 2024 — May 2025)
- **Supply:** 500 active listings across 148 sellers (avg 3.4 listings/seller)
- **Demand:** ~2,800 monthly unique visitors, 340 searches/day
- **Transactions:** 12 completed transactions/month ($1,840 GMV; avg order $153)
- **Key learning:** Sellers are engaged (78% log in weekly), but buyers browse and leave — conversion rate is 0.43% vs. 2–4% marketplace benchmark

### Hypotheses vs Reality

| Hypothesis | Expected (6 mo) | Actual | Verdict |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|---------|
| Local artisans will list products if we remove listing fees | 200+ listings | 500 listings | ✅ Exceeded |
| Buyers will transact if supply reaches 100+ listings | 50+ txn/month at 100 listings | 12 txn/month at 500 listings | ❌ Failed |
| Word-of-mouth from sellers will drive buyer traffic | 5,000 monthly visitors | 2,800 monthly visitors | ❌ Below target |
| Average order value will be $75–$125 | $75–$125 | $153 | ✅ Exceeded |
| Repeat purchase rate will reach 20% within 6 months | 20% | 4.2% | ❌ Far below |

### Evidence Assessment

**Supports continuing:**
- Strong seller engagement (78% weekly login, 500 listings growing 15%/mo)
- Higher-than-expected AOV ($153) suggests buyers who do purchase see value
- Seller NPS is +62; community aspect resonates

**Supports pivoting:**
- Buyer conversion rate (0.43%) is 5–10x below marketplace benchmarks
- Only 12 transactions/month after 6 months — nowhere near break-even (need 200+/mo)
- Repeat purchase rate (4.2%) suggests buyers treat it as a one-time novelty
- CAC for buyers is $47 via paid channels; LTV is ~$12 at current repeat rate

**Ambiguous:**
- Organic search traffic is growing 8%/mo — may compound, but slowly
- 23% of visitors add items to cart but don't check out — could be UX friction or trust issue

### Pivot Options Evaluated

| # | Option | Evidence For | Evidence Against | Effort | Risk |
|---|--------|-------------|-----------------|--------|------|
| 1 | **Pivot to B2B wholesale** — connect artisans to retail buyers (boutiques, gift shops) | AOV is high; sellers want volume; B2B has higher repeat rates | No B2B relationships; would need sales team; different product | High (3–4 mo) | Medium |
| 2 | **Pivot to curated subscription box** — monthly themed craft box shipped to subscribers | Eliminates buyer acquisition per-transaction; leverages seller supply | Requires logistics/fulfillment; subscription model untested | Medium (2–3 mo) | Medium |
| 3 | **Persevere with marketplace + fix conversion** — add reviews, guest checkout, trust badges; double down on SEO | 23% cart-add rate suggests latent demand; low cost to test | Conversion fixes alone unlikely to close a 5–10x gap; may be a trust problem not a UX problem | Low (4–6 wk) | Low |

### Recommendation

**Pivot to Option 2: Curated Subscription Box** (with marketplace as discovery layer)

**Top 3 reasons:**
1. **Solves the core problem:** Buyer acquisition is the bottleneck. A subscription model converts one acquisition into recurring revenue (LTV jumps from $12 to ~$150/year at $12.50/mo)
2. **Leverages our strength:** Seller supply and engagement are strong — we can curate from existing inventory without building a new supply side
3. **Testable cheaply:** We can validate demand with a landing page + 50-person pilot before building fulfillment infrastructure — estimated 4 weeks to first signal

### Decision Criteria

- [ ] Landing page conversion rate ≥ 5% from 1,000 targeted visitors — by June 1
- [ ] 50 paid subscribers ($12.50/mo) acquired within first 30 days — by July 1
- [ ] First box shipped with ≥ 4.0/5.0 satisfaction score — by July 15
- [ ] If all three pass: commit to full buildout. If any fail: evaluate Option 1 (B2B wholesale)

3. **Run $500 paid acquisition test to landing page** — Growth — by June 5
